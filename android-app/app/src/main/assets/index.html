Introduction to Mutual Funds in Pakistan

A mutual fund is an investment vehicle that pools money from multiple investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other securities. In Pakistan, mutual funds are heavily regulated by the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP), offering retail investors access to professional asset management.

Why Invest in Mutual Funds?

  • Professional Management: Qualified portfolio managers make investment decisions based on rigorous research.
  • Diversification: Instantly spread risk across dozens of companies or government securities, which is difficult with small capital on the stock exchange.
  • Liquidity: Open-end mutual funds can be bought or redeemed (sold back to the fund) on any business day. The AMC is bound to purchase them at the prevailing NAV.
  • Accessibility: You can start investing with as little as Rs. 500 or Rs. 5,000 depending on the AMC.

Core Asset Categories

Low Risk

Money Market Funds

Invest in short-term government securities (T-bills) and high-rated bank deposits. Capital protection is high, with stable annualized returns.

Medium Risk

Income / Debt Funds

Invest in government bonds (PIBs), corporate bonds (TFCs), and bank deposits. Yields are typically higher than money markets but carry credit and interest rate risk.

High Risk

Equity Funds

Invest directly in the Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX). Offers the highest long-term growth potential but is subject to daily stock market volatility.

Taxation, Withholding Tax, and Zakat Rules

Tax rules play a major role in your net returns. In Pakistan, tax rates differ significantly depending on your tax filer status (Active Taxpayer List - ATL) and the type of fund.

1. Capital Gains Tax (CGT)

When you redeem (sell) your mutual fund units, the profit is subject to CGT. Under current FBR rules, this tax is applied as a final tax liability:

Filer Status Equity (Stock) Funds Money Market & Income Funds
Active Filer (ATL) 15% on profit 15% on profit
Non-Filer 30% on profit 30% on profit

2. Dividend Withholding Tax (WHT)

If a fund distributes dividends, tax is deducted at source based on the fund's underlying asset allocation:

Filer Status Equity (Stock) Dividends Debt & Money Market Dividends
Active Filer (ATL) 15% WHT 25% WHT
Non-Filer 30% WHT 50% WHT

3. Zakat Deduction

Zakat is deducted at source at the rate of 2.5% of the redemption/face value of the investment on the 1st of Ramadan, unless you submit a Zakat Exemption Form.

Exemption Tip: If you wish to manage and pay Zakat yourself, you must submit a notarized CZ-50 affidavit (Zakat Exemption Form) to your AMC prior to the start of Ramadan.

4. Front-End and Back-End Loads

Many funds charge a sales commission:

  • Front-End Load: Deducted from your investment amount when you buy units (typically ranges from 1% to 2% for Equity, and 0% for Money Market).
  • Back-End Load: Deducted when you redeem your units (mostly 0% now, but some plans have early exit penalties).

How to Start Investing in Pakistan

Beginning your investment journey is straightforward. You can open an account directly with individual Asset Management Companies (AMCs) or use consolidated financial platforms.

Required Documents

  1. Computerized National Identity Card (CNIC / NICOP)
  2. Proof of Income / Profession (Salary slip, employment letter, or business letterhead)
  3. IBAN (International Bank Account Number) for direct payouts and redemptions
  4. Active Email Address and Mobile Number

Step-by-Step Account Opening Process

1

Select an AMC or Consolidated Platform

You can sign up directly with major AMCs (e.g. Meezan Funds, NBP Funds, UBL Funds, MCB-AH) online or use mobile apps like FinPocket or AMC apps (iSave, Meezan Funds app) which consolidate multiple funds.

2

Complete e-KYC (Digital Registration)

Fill out the online application, upload photos of your CNIC and proof of income, and record a short video selfie for biometrics validation.

3

Fund your Account

Transfer funds from your bank account via IBFT (Internet Banking) using the AMC's designated collection bank accounts. Enter the transaction reference on their app.

4

Purchase Units

Once your balance is updated, select the specific fund you want to invest in (e.g. Meezan Islamic Income Fund) and execute a purchase order. Units will be allocated to you based on that day's closing NAV.

Key Investment Terms Explained

Net Asset Value (NAV)
The market value of one unit of a mutual fund. It is calculated at the end of each business day by dividing the total net value of the fund's assets by the number of outstanding units.
Asset Management Company (AMC)
A financial company registered with SECP that manages mutual funds and portfolios on behalf of institutional and retail investors.
Cumulative Return
The overall percentage gain or loss generated by an investment over a specific period, without annualizing the results.
Annualized Return
The geometric mean of return rate computed on an annual basis, allowing comparison of funds that have been open for different lengths of time.
Expense Ratio
The annual fee charged by the fund to cover management costs, administrative fees, and trustee expenses, expressed as a percentage of the fund's average assets.
Trustee
An independent third-party custodian (commonly the Central Depository Company of Pakistan - CDC) that legally holds the assets of the mutual fund to protect the interests of unit holders.

Screener Score Calculation Methodology

To help you compare 500+ funds, our system calculates a custom Screener Score (out of 100) based on a weighted multi-factor quantitative ranking model. Instead of looking purely at returns, this score balances returns, entry loads, and fund quality ratings:

Weighted Scoring Breakdown:

  • 1-Year Return Yield (365d) 55% Weight
  • 3-Year Historical Yield 25% Weight
  • Low Sales Commission (Front/Back-End Load) 15% Weight
  • SECP/MUFAP Quality Rating (AMQC) 5% Weight

How the Scoring Works:

For each factor, the system calculates the fund's percentile rank compared to all other active funds. For example, if a fund's 1-year return is in the top 10% of all funds, it gets a high score for that factor. By factoring in sales loads and SECP ratings, the score prevents high-performance but high-fee/unrated funds from dominating the lists.
